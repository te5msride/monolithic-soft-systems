Monolithic Soft Systems

Co-design tools, materials, and fabrication strategies for next-generation robotics

Submit an Abstract/Poster View Agenda

Abstract

As the field of soft robotics continues to grow, simulation tools, sensing strategies, and fabrication methods are beginning to mature, providing a stronger foundation than ever before. Yet this progress brings a key question: should traditional robotics approaches be taken as examples, or do we need to rethink our design strategies to push the field forward?

Unlike rigid robots, soft robots face inherent challenges of material mismatch, creating unique demands for sensing, modeling, and control that are inseparable from fabrication and material choice. Bridging this gap calls for monolithic soft systems, where actuation, sensing, and structure are developed together through shared design strategies.

Part I of this workshop highlights enabling technologies — advances in simulation, embedded sensing, and fabrication. Part II focuses on interdisciplinary workflows: how teams fabricate, iterate, integrate, and simulate. By combining perspectives from engineering, biology, and materials science, the workshop aims to chart methods and collaborative practices that accelerate progress in soft robotics.
